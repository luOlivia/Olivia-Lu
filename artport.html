My experience



As a self taught artist, I started diving into the world of art at the age of 14.

Having since progressed from shaky and misshapen portraits, I'm currently exploring surrealism and different styles of art in my works.

The driving inspiration behind my work stems from unexplored worlds that surround us on Earth. The endless night sky and the swirling depths of the ocean always seems to find a place in my art.

I work with a range of mediums--dabbling in watercolor, pencil, ink, and acrylic. Aside from traditional mediums, I also have experience with digital art and graphic design.

I'm honored to have all the opportunities art has brought me.

In the past, I've been published 3 times and have sold my work at art shows.

In 2016, two of my photographs were on exhibit for a year at the National Building Museum in Washington DC. They were apart of the Investigating Where We Live exhibit, which I also helped design.

Recently, I've worked as a studio assistant at the Smithsonian's Hirshhorn Museum on the National Mall.

I currently share new artwork on my Instagram to over 8k followers.

Drowning

On ocean pollution and oil spills

This piece personifies the beauty of the ocean. At the surface level, many people don’t see the pollution that is lying underneath the vastness of the water or the glistening tides. All they see are their little “bubbles” of reality, shown in the circles surrounding the girl. Instead of the millions of plastic bottles or dangerous fish nets, they see the delicate fish or the swirling waves. But this beauty has been slowly encroached upon by human-created pollution. All the plastic, oil, and trash is beginning to engulf the girl and destroy the ocean habitats we’ve come to know and love. With its subtle details and thoughtful message, this drawing presents a pressing issue that needs to be addressed to create a healthy environment.

  • Medium: Ink on Paper
  • Size: 8.5 in x 11 in
  • Date: 2017
